ðŸ“Š Life Expectancy and Socioeconomic Indicators in 1970s U.S. States

This R-based project investigates the impact of regional socioeconomic factors on life expectancy using hierarchical Bayesian modeling with rjags. It combines state-level data from the 1970s with derived features like population density.

  • Explored correlations through scatter plots, correlation matrices, and choropleth maps
  • Identified murder rate and illiteracy as most negatively correlated with life expectancy
  • Fitted two Bayesian hierarchical linear models and compared via DIC
  • Concluded that murder, high school graduation, and frost days were most predictive
  • Validated model assumptions using residual plots and normality checks
